How to Create a Strong Instructor Profile

Your instructor profile helps clients decide whether you are the right fit before they send a message or choose you for a booking. A strong profile is clear, client-focused and easy to understand.

Become an instructor

Use a clear headshot

Choose a friendly, professional image where clients can clearly see you. This helps build trust before the first message.

Write a client-focused bio

Explain what you teach, who you work best with and what clients can expect from your sessions. Keep it warm, practical and specific.

List styles and service areas accurately

Accurate styles and service areas help you appear in relevant searches and reduce enquiries that are not a fit.

Keep replies professional

When replying to jobs, confirm availability, explain relevant experience and keep your message concise. Contact details should stay inside your account details, not chat messages.

Build reviews through completed bookings

Reviews help future clients understand your professionalism, preparation, reliability and teaching strengths.
